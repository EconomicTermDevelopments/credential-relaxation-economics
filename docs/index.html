credential relaxation

Field: Labor Economics | JEL Codes: I26, J24, J44

Definition

Credential relaxation describes the process by which employers reduce or eliminate formal educational and certification requirements for job positions in response to labor shortages, shifting toward skills-based assessment. This phenomenon represents a reversal of credential inflation, where positions that previously required bachelor's degrees or specific certifications are opened to candidates demonstrating equivalent competencies through alternative pathways.

Etymology

Compound noun combining "credential" (formal qualification, degree, or certificate) with "relaxation" (reduction or easing of requirements). The construction parallels established economic terminology like "monetary relaxation" and "regulatory relaxation," applying the same conceptual framework to hiring standards.

Usage Example

The tight labor market of 2022-2024 accelerated credential relaxation across technology and finance sectors, with 45% of companies eliminating degree requirements for previously restricted roles." educationdaily.
